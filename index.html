Overview

Modern robot autonomy increasingly depends on learning-based components, but fielded robots must meet real-time deadlines, run within compute/power limits, and behave robustly in closed-loop settings. This workshop brings together researchers and practitioners to discuss edge-deployed, latency-bounded AI pipelines spanning sensing, perception, decision-making, and control.

Topics of Interest

  • End-to-end latency budgeting (sensing → inference → decision → control)
  • Resource-aware architectures (edge-only vs offload/nearby compute)
  • Efficient AI pipelines (quantization, distillation, pruning, caching) as systems tools
  • Runtime monitoring, safety fallbacks, uncertainty-aware decision-making
  • Tool-integrated autonomy (ROS2 integration, planners, SLAM, safety monitors)
  • Benchmarking & evaluation: latency/energy/thermal + robustness in real deployments
